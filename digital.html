Ethical Digital Citizenship

Chosen Topic: The role of ethical language in digital citizenship to combat misinformation.

Format: Research Report & Digital Awareness Campaign

Context and Significance

The rise of social media has increased the spread of false information. This affects everything from elections to public health. Ethical communication helps build a more trustworthy digital world.

Social, Ethical, and Technological Concerns

  • Social: Misinformation causes confusion and division.
  • Ethical: Digital citizens must verify sources and communicate truthfully.
  • Technological: AI bots and algorithms amplify misinformation, making truth harder to find.

Global and Personal Connections

Globally, false information about COVID-19 treatments had serious health impacts. Personally, many people have shared misleading news without knowing it was false—highlighting the need for media literacy.

Real-World Examples

  • MIT (2018) found false news spreads six times faster than true news on Twitter.
  • Snopes and Reuters Fact Check help counter misinformation with verified facts.
  • UNESCO's “Think Before You Share” teaches responsible digital communication.

Key Findings

Ethical language is essential for digital trust and accountability. Upholding amanah requires users to verify, clarify, and share responsibly.
